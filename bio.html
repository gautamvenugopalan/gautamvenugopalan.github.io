Education & Work Experience

I grew up and went to school in Singapore. For my undergraduate degree, I attended the Indian Institute of Technology in Madras, India. During my summers, I got the opportunity to intern at various labs around the world, allowing me to broaden and sharpen my skills as an experimental physicist. My first foray into the world of gravitational wave astronomy was working at GEO600 in Hannover. I found the science of LIGO extremely cool, so I interned the next summer at the Caltech 40m prototype interferometer, where I'd eventually end up pursuing my Ph. D. I got a chance to do something a little different working with the Attosecond group at the Max-Planck Institute for Quantum Optics in Munich in the summer of 2014. Then, for my masters thesis, I analyzed data from the Belle experiment in Tsukuba, studying a decay channel sensitive to the CP-violating phase, $\phi_3$.

I spent grad school (2015-2021) at Caltech in Pasadena, working with the LIGO group. This was a truly exciting time of many firsts (first direct detection, first multi-messenger event, etc.), and I got to learn from some of the best experimental physicists and engineers at Caltech and the LIGO observatories. During this time, I also got to visit and commission the underground Japanese gravitational wave detector, KAGRA, near Toyama.

Currently, I work at Stanford University as a postdoctoral researcher with the Gratta group, working on table-top scale optomechanical experiments that use optically levitated force sensors to search for new physics.
