Optimal dielectric coating design

Many precision measurement applications, such as telescope mirrors, lasers and optical cavities, require dielectric coatings that satisfy multiple design requirements. While the equations governing the reflectivity of multi-layer films are straightforward, algorithms to simultaneously satisfy multiple objectives are often only available through commercial software with expensive licensing fees. I developed a python based software suite that can efficiently evaluate optimal designs given multiple requirements. The algorithm was successfully applied it to several case-studies, and could be run on a modern laptop (without having to resort to a supercomputer cluster) for complex problems with up to $\mathcal{O(100)}$ dielectric layers.

An interesting extension of the technique was to the inverse problem - that is, the algorithm was efficiently able to compute the coating structure given a measurement of its spectral reflectivity. We successfully validated this on a moderately complex design, that of an Advanced LIGO mirror with 38 dielectric layers, for which the thickness of the individual layers was known. The algorithm successfully recovered these thicknesses to reasonable accuracy, again without having to rely on supercomputing resources.
