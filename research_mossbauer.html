Mössbauer spectroscopy as a tool to search for new forces / fields

A powerful technique to measure a weak force is to couple it to an oscillator - then, by measuring the response of the oscillator, we can deduce the force that produced said response. A shift in the oscillator's resonant frequency is one kind of measurable response. The precision of the measurement is then limited by the narrowness of the resonance, known as the quality factor (Q). The Mössbauer spectroscopy technique offers up some of the highest Q resonances known. While it was discovered in 1958, it has only been used for testing fundamental physics sparingly (most famously in the Pound-Rebka experiment to measure the gravitational redshift of the Earth). It has recently been proposed as a tool to search for new forces or fields, which have an interaction range of $\sim 1-100\,\mathrm{nm}$.
